AP Biology

Course Syllabus

Advanced Placement Biology curriculum reflects a college introductory biology course taken by biology majors. After demonstrating proficiency on the AP Biology Exam, some college freshman may earn college credit or can enroll in upper-level courses in biology. AP Biology will include topics regularly covered in a required college introductory biology course. Primary emphasis will be on developing an understanding of biological concepts; a grasp of science as a process rather than as an accumulation of facts; personal experience in scientific inquiry; recognition of unifying themes that integrate the major topics of biology; and the application of biological knowledge and critical thinking to environmental and social concerns. At Edison High School, AP Biology is an elective science course for students who are highly motivated and have a strong interest in science. Major topics of study include biochemistry, cells, cellular metabolism, genetics, evolution, classification, organism structure/function, and ecology. As in a college biology course, AP Biology is designed to have both a lecture and a laboratory component. Labs conducted by AP Biology students are the equivalent of those experienced by college biology students. At the completion of this course, students are expected to take the College Board’s scheduled AP Biology exam in May. To assist students in their preparation for this test, the format of the assessments used throughout this course are similar to the AP Biology exam. This course is fast-paced, rigorous, and demands more at-home study time than the standard college-prep courses at Edison High School. The College Board determines AP course content and pacing. For sample questions and a detailed course outline see the College Board’s AP Biology website.
